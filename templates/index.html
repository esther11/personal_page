Striving for Eudaimonia

Eudaimonia, the Greek word εὐδαιμονία, is commonly translated as "welfare". To quote Brian Christian's The Most Human Human, Eudaimonia "means something along the lines of 'well-being of spirit'" - "flourishing", a subjective life experience.

"Critical to eudaimonia is Arete ἀρετή - 'arete' - translated as 'fulfillment of purpose'... Flowers are meant to bloom; knives are meant to cut; what are we meant to do?"

Having witnessed and heard of abundant stories, what is important is living one's own. Here's my story, with much to cherish and little to complain about on my way to figuring out who I want to be, where is the boundary of my capacity, and what I am meant to do.
